Railroad Labor Dispute Summary

In late 2022, Congress intervened to avert a potential railroad strike affecting over 100,000 workers across major freight railroads. Key points from the Congressional Research Service report:

  • Legislative Action: The House passed two bills (H.J.Res. 100 and H.Con.Res. 119) to impose a September 2022 tentative agreement and propose paid sick days
  • Labor Conditions: Railroad employment had declined by 20% (40,000 jobs) since 2018 due to coal industry changes and "precision scheduled railroading" (PSR) efficiency measures
  • Key Issues: Workers demanded better sick leave policies (only 3 unpaid days in proposal), opposed aggressive PSR implementations, and sought crew size protections
  • Legal Framework: Negotiations were governed by the Railway Labor Act, with President Biden establishing an Emergency Board in August 2022 after failed mediation
  • Outcome: While 8 of 12 unions ratified agreements, 4 rejected them, bringing the system to the brink of a December 9 strike before congressional intervention

"UNION" Documentary Summary

The documentary "UNION" chronicles the Amazon Labor Union's (ALU) groundbreaking organizing efforts at Amazon's JFK8 fulfillment center in Staten Island. Key aspects covered:

  • Grassroots Movement: Follows former Amazon employee Chris Smalls and colleagues building an independent union from the ground up
  • Working Conditions: Documents the demanding productivity quotas, safety concerns, and anti-union tactics that motivated the organizing drive
  • Historic Victory: Captures the April 2022 election where workers voted to form Amazon's first U.S. union despite corporate opposition
  • Challenges: Explores the ongoing struggles for recognition and contract negotiations after the initial win
  • Broader Impact: Highlights how the ALU's success inspired organizing efforts at other Amazon facilities nationwide

Starbucks Workers United Summary

The Starbucks Workers United movement represents one of the most significant unionization efforts in the service industry's recent history. Key developments:

  • Movement Origins: Began in August 2021 when Buffalo, NY baristas filed for union elections, citing inconsistent scheduling, understaffing, and wage concerns
  • Rapid Expansion: Grew from the first successful union vote in December 2021 to over 400 unionized stores nationwide by 2024
  • Organizing Tactics: Workers United has employed innovative strategies including social media campaigns and "sip-ins" to build public support
  • Corporate Resistance: Faces ongoing challenges with Starbucks' aggressive anti-union tactics, including store closures and withholding benefits from unionized locations
  • Recent Progress: After a 2023 policy shift, Starbucks has begun bargaining with over 400 unionized stores, marking a new phase in the movement
