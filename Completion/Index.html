Modern Labor Movements Timeline

Railroad Labor Dispute
Amazon Unionization
Starbucks Unionization
November 2019

Rail Negotiations Begin

Contract negotiations start between rail unions and carriers, focusing on working conditions and benefits.

March 2020

Chris Smalls Leads Walkout

Amazon worker Chris Smalls organizes Staten Island walkout over COVID safety concerns, leading to his firing and eventual union organizing.

August 2022

Presidential Emergency Board

Biden establishes emergency board to investigate rail dispute as negotiations stall.

December 2021

First Starbucks Unionizes

Workers at a Buffalo, NY Starbucks vote to unionize, the first corporate-owned store to do so in decades.

April 2022

ALU Wins Historic Election

Amazon Labor Union wins election at JFK8 warehouse, creating Amazon's first US union.

September 2022

Tentative Agreement Reached

Unions and carriers reach deal hours before strike deadline, but 4 unions later reject it.

2022-2023

Rapid Expansion

Over 400 Starbucks stores vote to unionize across the US, despite corporate opposition.

December 2022

Congress Imposes Contract

Congress intervenes to prevent strike, imposing terms rejected by some unions.

2023

Contract Negotiations Stall

Amazon continues to resist recognizing ALU, leading to protracted legal battles.

2023-2024

Bargaining Begins

After policy shift, Starbucks begins contract negotiations with unionized stores.
